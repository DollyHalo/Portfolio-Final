La Catrina

I designed this wearable art as a momento mori piece, a reminder to live life to the fullest.

La Catrina is a sacred symbol that represents in Mexican culture, the choice people make to celebrate the lives of those that they lost instead of focusing that they're gone forever.

The structure of the body is constructed with wire, and painted in layers of tissue paper. Features large avant garde sleeves, exaggerated collar, LEDs incorporated in the sleeves like lanterns, and embellished with tissue butterflies and recycled mardi gras beads. This ribcage is constructed with wire, paper, spray paint, and acrylic.
